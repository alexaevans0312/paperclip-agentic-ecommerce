# Source Context

This project is based on Alexa Evans' real Squish business plan and current operating work, not a speculative hackathon toy.

## Business foundation

Squish is a pre-launch skincare ecommerce company starting with premium visible hydrocolloid pimple patches.

Core investor-plan thesis:

> Squish is a focused wedge into a growing skincare behaviour: visible, confident, replenishable acne care. The first SKU is simple by design; the operating model is where the leverage compounds.

## First SKU

- Premium visible hydrocolloid pimple patches.
- 36-40 count pack.
- Target retail price: $16.
- Hydrocolloid-only first launch.
- No fragrance, no acne-drug actives.
- Claim lane: absorb fluid, protect from picking, support an ideal healing environment, designed for surface whiteheads.
- Visual differentiation through shapes, colour, and packaging.

## Product roadmap

1. Visible hydrocolloid shapes.
2. Invisible daytime patch.
3. Cluster/body patch.
4. Aftercare/dark-spot appearance patch.
5. Active early-stage patch.
6. Microdart premium patch.

This roadmap creates a patch wardrobe: more customer jobs, higher AOV, better repeat purchase, and clearer SKU expansion.

## Business model

Revenue path:

```text
Social/content/creator discovery
  -> Shopify/TikTok Shop/Amazon product page
  -> first purchase, ideally bundle-led
  -> post-purchase education
  -> replenishment reminder
  -> review/UGC loop
  -> repeat purchase or second SKU expansion
```

Primary levers:

- Pack sales at ~$16 retail.
- Bundles to push AOV to $30-36.
- Subscriptions/refills after repeat behaviour is proven.
- Seasonal drops and limited colourways.
- Future SKU expansion.

## Financial model anchors

- Net revenue per pack: $15.20 after discount/returns allowance.
- Estimated landed COGS per pack: $3.20.
- Fulfillment per order estimate: $5.50.
- Payment processing proxy: 2.9% + $0.30.
- Launch AOV target: $28-32 through bundles.
- Blended CAC target: $14-18 early, rising only if LTV supports it.
- 90-day repeat target: 25%+.
- Safer investor raise target: ~$350k.

Base-case projections from the investor model:

| Year | Net revenue | EBITDA before tax | LTV:CAC |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 2027 | $1.27M | $122k | 2.19 |
| 2028 | $4.60M | $824k | 2.43 |
| 2029 | $13.93M | $3.43M | 2.74 |

## Agentic operating model

Squish is designed as an agentic ecommerce company: one founder/operator supported by AI agents and automated workflows.

Agent-supported functions:

- Market research and social listening.
- Competitor monitoring.
- Supplier tracking and sample scorecards.
- Compliance claim-bank management.
- Content calendar and creative briefs.
- Email/SMS flow drafts.
- Customer review mining.
- Financial model updates.
- Shopify/product-data hygiene.
- Investor reporting and weekly operating dashboards.

## Why Paperclip exists

Paperclip is the orchestration layer that turns this operating model into a repeatable system. It is not an “AI marketing assistant.” It is a business operations layer for ecommerce execution.

The hackathon demo should prove that the agent can run a real slice of the Squish operating system:

- produce a compliant acquisition plan,
- structure a monetisable offer,
- identify required commerce infrastructure,
- prepare Stripe-governed spend/provisioning actions,
- keep the founder in control of sensitive decisions,
- leave behind artifacts that the business can actually reuse.
