# Narration Script

Ecommerce founders do not need another AI that writes captions.

They need an operator that understands the business: what can earn, what costs money, what needs approval, and what cannot be said in public.

This is Paperclip Agentic Ecommerce, built on Hermes Agent for Squish, a real pre-launch skincare brand launching premium visible hydrocolloid pimple patches.

The founder gives Paperclip a goal: launch the first bundle-led offer, keep CAC under eighteen dollars, and make sure every claim stays cosmetic-safe.

Cleo, running through Hermes, breaks that goal into specialist workstreams.

Mira builds the offer and funnel. Ledger checks the unit economics. Vera reviews skincare claims and FTC disclosure risk. Piper structures the creator workflow. Nova prepares Stripe-governed spend and provisioning actions. Nemo packages the plan for Nemotron 3 Ultra review.

The important part is the control layer.

Paperclip can prepare actions that earn, spend, and provision infrastructure — but it does not silently move money. Stripe actions are queued with a business reason, estimated cost, spend cap, environment, approval status, and verification command.

Nemotron 3 Ultra acts as the high-context reviewer, stress-testing the ecommerce plan, the payment process, the compliance risks, and the missing dependencies before anything sensitive executes.

The output is not a chat response. It is an operating packet: offer structure, funnel, unit economics, claim-safe copy, creator plan, Stripe action ledger, and founder next actions.

Squish is the first use case. The larger pattern is agentic ecommerce: one founder, specialised agents, governed spend, and a business system that compounds over time.
